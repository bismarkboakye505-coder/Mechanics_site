Inclined Planes

A block of mass m on an incline of angle θ experiences weight components: mg sinθ (parallel to slope) and mg cosθ (perpendicular). With friction coefficient μ, friction is μmg cosθ.

INCLINE PLANES


Equations of Motion

  • Smooth plane: a = g sinθ
  • Rough plane, sliding down: a = g(sinθ − μ cosθ)
  • Rough, pulled up with force P: resolve forces and apply ΣF = ma.
  • No motion criterion: tanθ ≤ μ
Example (Smooth):
m = 4 kg, θ = 30° → a = g sin30° = 4.9 m/s²
Example (Rough):
m = 6 kg, θ = 25°, μ = 0.2 → a ≈ 1.20 m/s²

Free-Body Diagram Tips

  1. Draw mg vertically downward.
  2. Resolve into mg sinθ (down slope) and mg cosθ (normal).
  3. Add normal reaction R and friction opposing motion.
