Pulley Systems

Pulleys reduce effort or change direction of forces. The simplest models are studied in mechanics: fixed pulleys, movable pulleys, and the Atwood machine.

1) Fixed Pulley

Changes direction of force; mechanical advantage = 1.

2) Movable Pulley

Shares load between two ropes; mechanical advantage = 2.

3) Atwood Machine

Two masses m₁ and m₂ connected by a string over a frictionless pulley. Tension T is the same throughout the rope.

THE PULLEY SYSTEM


Equations

  • Acceleration: a = (m₂ − m₁)g / (m₁ + m₂)
  • Tension: T = 2m₁m₂g / (m₁ + m₂)
Example:
m₁ = 3 kg, m₂ = 5 kg → a = 2.45 m/s², T ≈ 36.75 N
Check:
If m₁ = m₂, then a = 0 (equilibrium).
