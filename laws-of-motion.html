Laws of Motion

Newton’s laws describe how forces affect motion. They connect cause (forces) and effect (motion) and are foundational in mechanics.

1) First Law (Inertia)

A body continues in a state of rest or uniform motion in a straight line unless compelled to change by an external force.

  • Zero net force ⇒ zero acceleration.
  • Examples: passenger lurching in a bus, a puck gliding on ice.
Example: A 2 kg cart moving at 3 m/s with zero net force continues at 3 m/s.

2) Second Law (F = ma)

Acceleration is proportional to net force and inversely proportional to mass. F = ma, N = kg·m/s².

Example: 5 kg block, F = 20 N → a = 4 m/s²
Example: Car m = 1200 kg, F = 3000 N → a = 2.5 m/s²

3) Third Law (Action–Reaction)

Every action has an equal and opposite reaction. Forces occur in pairs, acting on different bodies.

  • Walking: foot pushes ground back, ground pushes forward.
  • Rocket: gas pushed backward, rocket forward.
