Forces

A force is a push or pull that can change the state of motion of a body. It has both magnitude and direction, and is measured in Newtons (N).

Types of Forces

Contact Forces

  • Friction: opposes motion between surfaces.
  • Normal Reaction: force perpendicular to a surface.
  • Tension: pulling force in strings or ropes.
  • Applied Force: push or pull by an agent.

Non-Contact Forces

  • Gravitational Force: attraction between masses, weight = mg.
  • Electrostatic Force: between charged particles.
  • Magnetic Force: due to magnetic fields.

ILLUSTRATIONS OF FORCES


Free-Body Diagrams (FBD)

  1. Isolate the object of interest.
  2. Show all forces acting on it as arrows from the center.
  3. Resolve angled forces into components (Fx, Fy).
  4. Apply ΣF = ma along each axis.

Worked Examples

Example 1:
A 10 kg block pulled with 40 N horizontally, friction 10 N.
Net force = 30 N, a = F/m = 3 m/s².
Example 2:
Three forces in equilibrium form a closed triangle. ΣF = 0.
